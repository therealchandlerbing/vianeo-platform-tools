**Example:**

The current validation status reveals a **critical disconnect between technical and commercial validation**. While the technology has achieved TRL 4 with proven lab results, customer validation is preliminary at best.

**Technical Validation:** Strong. Lab demonstrations successful, powder characteristics confirmed, production process proven at small scale.

**Commercial Validation:** Weak. Only 12 of 30 target interviews completed. Primary segments (manufacturers, strategic sectors) largely unvalidated. All needs remain hypotheses rather than customer-confirmed priorities.

**Implications for Business Model Confidence:** Moderate to low. We can build the product, but we don't yet know if customers will buy it at prices that make business sense. Revenue projections lack foundation. Go-to-market strategy based on assumptions.

**Required Next Steps:** Complete remaining 18 interviews within 8 weeks. Secure 3-5 pilot testing agreements. Validate willingness to pay premium vs. import pricing. Do not proceed with major capital investment until commercial validation strengthens.

## Formatting Best Practices

1. **Emphasis on Gaps:** Make validation needs obvious with callout boxes and colored badges
2. **Actionable Recommendations:** Use callout boxes to highlight priorities
3. **Honest Assessment:** Use warning boxes for critical issues
4. **Decision-Ready:** Use risk badges and clear bottom-line recommendation
5. **Visual Hierarchy:** Use color-coded headers and badges to guide reader
6. **Scannable:** Use callout boxes for key decisions and recommendations
7. **Professional but Urgent:** Balance professional design with urgency of gaps
