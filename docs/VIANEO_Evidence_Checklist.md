# Vianeo Market Maturity Assessment: Evidence Checklist

## Purpose

Use this checklist to verify you have the required evidence before assigning scores. For each question, check what evidence exists in your materials.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

### Before Scoring

1. **Read the question**
2. **Review the evidence requirements below**
3. **Check your materials** for the specific evidence
4. **If evidence exists**: Assign appropriate score (3-5) and cite source
5. **If evidence is missing**: Assign Score 1 and specify what's needed

### Evidence Quality Standards

**External Evidence** (Required for Score 3+):
- Customer interviews (documented with names/dates)
- User testing results (specific feedback)
- Expert validation (named experts)
- Market research (cited reports)
- Financial testing (actual customer responses)

**Internal Evidence** (Max Score 2):
- Team assumptions
- Internal analysis
- Plans for future validation
- Beliefs about market/customers

## Before Submitting Assessment

Review this final checklist:

- [ ] Every Score 4-5 has specific external evidence cited
- [ ] Every Score 1 specifies exactly what evidence is needed
- [ ] Evidence sources are named (document, interview, report)
- [ ] Numbers are used where available (X interviews, Y customers)
- [ ] No vague references ("team believes", "planning to")
- [ ] All evidence is external, not internal assumptions
- [ ] Evidence dates are recent (not outdated)
- [ ] Evidence is verifiable if needed

---

## Evidence Quality Guidelines

### High-Quality Evidence
- Documented customer interviews with dates
- User testing results with specific feedback
- Named experts providing validation
- Cited market research reports
- Financial testing with real customer responses
- Signed agreements or letters of intent
- Actual usage data or metrics

### Low-Quality Evidence
- "Team believes"
- "We think"
- "Planning to"
- "Will do"
- Internal opinions
- Assumptions
- Hopes or aspirations

### Borderline Evidence (Score 2 Maximum)
- 1-3 customer conversations
- Informal feedback
- Secondary research only
- Single source validation
- Outdated information (>1 year)
