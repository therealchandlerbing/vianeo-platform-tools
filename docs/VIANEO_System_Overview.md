## Key Principles Across All Documents

### 1. Evidence Over Assumptions
- Score what exists, not what's planned
- Require external validation for high scores
- Default to Score 1 when evidence is missing

### 2. Specificity Over Generality
- Cite exact sources
- Use numbers when available
- Name specific documents
- Avoid vague language

### 3. Action Over Judgment
- Every Score 1 specifies what's needed
- Lower scores show where to focus
- Gaps are opportunities for improvement
- Assessment drives specific next steps

### 4. Honesty Over Optimism
- Don't inflate scores to look good
- Acknowledge information gaps
- Distinguish validated from assumed
- Be transparent about limitations

## Critical Success Factors

### Questions That Cannot Be Score 1

For business to proceed with confidence, these require validation:

**Q7**: Must have 5+ interviews per segment (minimum Score 3)
**Q13**: Problem must be validated by multiple people (minimum Score 3)
**Q19**: Revenue model must be tested with customers (minimum Score 2)
**Q22**: Must test with customers per segment (minimum Score 2)

If any of these are Score 1, assessment should recommend pausing development to gather this evidence.

## Dimension Interdependencies

Understanding how dimensions connect helps identify systemic issues:

**Legitimacy ‚Üí Desirability**
- Problem definition shapes user needs investigation
- Weak Legitimacy suggests Desirability may be built on wrong foundation

**Desirability ‚Üí Acceptability**
- User segments indicate relevant ecosystem players
- Strong Desirability without Acceptability = adoption barriers

**Acceptability ‚Üí Feasibility**
- Ecosystem mapping identifies potential partners
- Regulatory requirements shape technical specifications

**Feasibility ‚Üí Viability**
- Technical approach determines cost structure
- Implementation timeline affects financial projections

**Viability ‚Üí All Others**
- Business model sustainability validates overall concept
- Weak Viability questions earlier assumptions

## Common Assessment Patterns

### Pattern 1: Technology in Search of Problem
- High Feasibility (team can build)
- Low Legitimacy (problem not validated)
- Low Desirability (no customer research)
‚Üí Stop building, start interviewing

### Pattern 2: Great Idea, No Validation
- High Legitimacy (clear problem)
- Low Desirability (assumed not validated)
- Low Viability (untested business model)
‚Üí Validate before building

### Pattern 3: Validated Concept, Weak Execution
- High Legitimacy & Desirability
- Low Feasibility (resource gaps)
- Low Viability (no revenue path)
‚Üí Build team and business model

### Pattern 4: Ready for Scale
- All dimensions ‚â• 3.5
- Few remaining gaps
- Evidence well-documented
‚Üí Address minor gaps, prepare for growth

## Quality Standards

### Excellent Assessment
- All 29 questions scored with specific evidence
- Every Score 4-5 cites external validation
- Every Score 1 specifies exact evidence needed
- Justifications are clear and concise
- Dimension scores calculated correctly
- Action recommendations are specific and prioritized
- Follow-up schedule established

### Adequate Assessment
- All questions scored
- Most high scores have evidence
- Most Score 1s specify needs
- Basic justifications provided
- Scores calculated
- Some action recommendations
- Next steps identified

### Needs Improvement
- Vague justifications
- High scores without evidence
- Score 1s without stating needs
- Missing calculations
- No action plan
- Generic recommendations

## Recommended Workflows

### For Internal Team Use
1. Founder completes self-assessment
2. Team member conducts independent assessment
3. Compare results and discuss gaps
4. Agree on consensus scores
5. Create joint action plan

### For Advisor/Mentor Evaluation
1. Request materials in advance
2. Complete assessment independently
3. Schedule review meeting
4. Walk through findings
5. Collaborate on action plan
6. Set follow-up assessment date

### For Investor Due Diligence
1. Request comprehensive materials
2. Complete thorough assessment
3. Verify external evidence
4. Identify deal-breakers (any dimension below threshold)
5. Request additional evidence for gaps
6. Reassess after evidence provided

### For Accelerator/Incubator Selection
1. Standardize materials requested
2. Multiple evaluators assess independently
3. Compare scores for consistency
4. Discuss significant disagreements
5. Create consensus assessment
6. Use for selection decisions

## Getting Support

### If Scoring is Unclear
- Review VIANEO_29Question_Quick_Reference.md
- Check VIANEO_Evidence_Checklist.md
- Consult example patterns in Workflow Guide
- When in doubt, default to lower score and gather evidence

### If Evidence is Missing
- Score 1 and specify what's needed
- Don't estimate or assume
- Create plan to gather evidence
- Reassess after evidence collection

### If Results Are Surprising
- Review for consistency across questions
- Check calculation accuracy
- Verify evidence quality
- Discuss with team or advisor
- Consider whether results reveal blind spots
