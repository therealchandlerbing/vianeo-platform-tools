### Interview Best Practices

**Subsection Header:** (12pt, Bold, Color: #17A2B8)
```
Interview Best Practices
```

**Format:** Numbered list with bold headers and explanatory text

**Example:**

1. **Listen More, Talk Less (80/20 rule)**

   You should listen 80% of the time, talk 20%. Resist the urge to pitch or explain your solution. Comfortable silence means they're thinking - don't fill it. The more they talk, the more you learn.

2. **Ask Open-Ended Questions**

   Use "Tell me about..." not "Do you...?" Use "How" and "Why" questions to encourage stories. Avoid yes/no questions that close conversation. Open-ended questions reveal deeper insights.

3. **Dig Deeper with Follow-Ups**

   When something interesting comes up, probe further:
   â€¢ "Why is that?"
   â€¢ "Tell me more"
   â€¢ "Can you give me an example?"
   â€¢ "What else?"

   The best insights come from follow-up questions.

4. **Listen for Problems, Not Solutions**

   Don't pitch your solution until Phase 5. Understand their needs first. When they propose solutions, note it, but dig for the underlying need. You want to understand the problem, not validate their solution ideas.

5. **Validate with Specifics**

   "That's interesting" â‰  validation. Get concrete:
   â€¢ "How much would you pay?" (specific number)
   â€¢ "Would you test this?" (yes/no commitment)
   â€¢ "When would you make a decision?" (specific date)

   Vague enthusiasm isn't validation. Specific commitments are.

6. **Don't Sell, Learn**

   Frame this as research, not a sales call. You're there to understand, not convince. Their honest feedback is more valuable than their politeness. Bad news now saves you from building the wrong thing.

7. **End with Referrals**

   Always ask: "Who else should I talk to?" Warm introductions >> cold outreach. Ask for 2-3 specific names. Build your network systematically through referrals.
